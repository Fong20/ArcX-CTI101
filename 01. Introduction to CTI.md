# 01. Introduction to CTI

## Definition
- Cyber threat intelligence (CTI) is the gathering of information from various sources about current or potential threats to an organisation.
- However, CTI can also be defined as the a structured analysis of the threat, and the structure can be dynamically changed depending on the situation

## Objectives
- Cybersecurity in general is defensive in nature, and CTI is no exception. It is not the role of CTI to defend systems from attack, but to advise on what the best defense against the attacker is to defenders and help defenders move at equal speed with the attacker to mitigate the threat.
- Hence, communication between people / cybersecurity specializations is also key to success in CTI.

The purpose of cyber threat intelligence is to give organisations an in-depth understanding of the threats that pose the greatest risk to their infrastructure and devise a plan to protect their day-to-day business. CTI Analysts strive to provide as much actionable intelligence as possible.

Some of the key reasons that organisations use cyber threat intelligence could also be

- Identify and assess potential threats to their networks and systems.
- Enhance their overall security posture by proactively taking measures to prevent attacks.
- Improve incident response efforts by having up-to-date information about known threats.
- Prioritise resources for the mitigation of high-risk vulnerabilities.
- Monitor external sources for signs of a potential breach or attack.
- Stay informed about the tactics, techniques, and procedures used by malicious actors.
  
## Data Information Intelligence Pyramid
Consists of 3 main stages and 2 processes

### Stages
1. Data = simple facts that tend to be available in large volume. Eg: IP addresses, logs
2. Information = data that is processed to provide a useful outcome
3. Intelligence = produced from the processing and analysis of the information, and can be used to inform decision making

### Processes
1. Collection = the process of converting data to information
2. Analysis = the process of converting information to intelligence

## Usage of CTI in Organizations
CTI is used in daily business processes, which can be divided into three levels

1. Strategic Level = the board and senior decision makers
2. Operational level = Daily operations that support the business. Eg: SOC operations, security control application
3. Tactical level = Technical operations. Eg: threat hunting, log analysis etc

Note: the type of intelligence provided varies based on the level

## Indicatiors of Compromise (IOC)
Indicatiors of Compromise (IOC) are pieces of forensic data, such as data found in system log entires or files, that identify potentially malicious activity on a system or network.
